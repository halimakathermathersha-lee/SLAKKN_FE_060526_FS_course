Background

Elections to state legislative assemblies in India are usually held once in five years, and the members of the legislative assembly are directly elected to serve five year terms from single-member constituencies. The previous assembly elections were held in April 2021 to elect the 234 members of the 16th Tamil Nadu Assembly, and the tenure of the assembly ends on 10 May 2026.[5] In the previous election, the Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (DMK)-led Secular Progressive Alliance (SPA) formed the state government after winning 159 of the 234 seats, and M. K. Stalin sworn in as the chief minister.[6][7] The All India Anna Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (AIADMK), which won 66 seats, became the principal opposition party and its leader Edappadi K. Ramadoss.[15][16] After Anbumani was recognised as the official leader of the PMK, Ramadoss formed a splinter faction of the PMK.[17] Two members–T. K. Amulkandasami (AIADMK) and K. Parties and Alliance

The Tamil Nadu Election 2026 featured strong competition between major parties and alliances. TVK entered the election with its alliance partners and gained huge public support. The DMK alliance included parties like Congress and IUML, while the AIADMK alliance worked with PMK and other regional parties. Throughout the election, these alliances campaigned across Tamil Nadu to attract voters with different promises and development plans. The election results showed the importance of alliances in shaping the political future of the state.
