Short Story:“The Boy Who Brought Back the Lights"

Once there was a poor boy named Arjun in a small village. He was not good at studies, and everyone in class laughed at him. Even teachers thought he would never become successful. But Arjun had one special habit — whenever something broke in the house, he tried to fix it. Radios, fans, clocks… he opened them carefully and learned how they worked. One day, a big storm hit the village.


Electricity went off for two days. People were struggling. Arjun spent the whole night trying to repair an old generator lying in a shop. Everyone thought he would fail.After many hours, the generator suddenly started. The lights in the village came back. For the first time, people stopped laughing at him. Years later, Arjun became an engineer who repaired machines for entire towns. When someone asked him how he became successful even after failing in school, he smiled and said: “Marks showed what I memorized. My hands showed what I learned.”

Moral: Being weak in one thing does not mean you are useless in everything
